Description:

HTSeq-count is very similar to its alternative featureCounts: both require the provided input alignment files to be in BAM or SAM format, as well as a genome feature file in GTF or GFF format. Although these tools return similar results, counts can vary considerably in case of paired-ended data. FeatureCounts is faster than its counterpart HTSeq-count, and its count table takes into account feature overlaps. On the contrary, HTSeq-count is slower and more consirvative in terms of counting fragments whose alignment overlaps multiple features. Compared to featureCounts, HTSeq-count is older, and therefore widely used in the community.

DESeq2 is a tool to carry out differential gene expression analysis. This tool computes ranking and visualization of count data for hypothesis testing under multiple sampling conditions.
